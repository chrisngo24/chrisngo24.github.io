About Me

I have been attracted to technology and creative design my entire life. I examine things in its current form and my mind will attempt to design a better version of it. In the last 5 years, Iâ€™ve come to realize that I was witnessing the world evolve so rapidly through the greatest technological invention of my time; the internet. The state-of-the-art world I had envisioned as a kid had become reality.

I never wanted to just be an outside observer while technology takes leaps forward without me. I always wanted to be part of it but I was not aware how much of the tech field I still had not been exposed to. So I started researching in-depth and quickly discovered that full-stack web development (with all of its vastness and intangible implications) satisfies my propensity for technology and design simultaneously.

Upon studying web development and learning about the field, I noticed how nuanced and intricate the craft is. I am fascinated by the adaptable nature of software development as a tool that allows it to interface with nearly any idea or concept and yield various creative solutions. In addition to my programming skills, I bring management, customer service, and problem-solving skills from my prior experience in the pharmaceutical industry. I know I can apply those skills and many more in my new field due to the diverse nature of web development.

I believe that technology should compliment and elevate our quality of life in big and small ways, while being attainable, usable, and comprehensible for all people. I want to build intelligent forward-thinking tech experiences that connect our minds, transport us, help us with essential daily life stuff, or just for having fun. I see boundless potential in developing for the web and it makes me astronomically excited to be able to contribute.

Capstone

CONVEY is an app that combines search, research and learning elements into one website, specifically for words and languages. Users are able to view established data from the Wordnik Dictionary API and data created by other users as well. This allows the user to be efficient when doing research while simultaneously encouraging diversified language learning. The backend was built with Ruby on Rails and the frontend was built with VueJS.
